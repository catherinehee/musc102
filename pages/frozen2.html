Frozen II

2019

Phase II: The Modern Era

Overview: Negotiating Power Differentials

Following criticism of the original Frozen (2013) for its superficial use of generalized Scandinavian motifs, Disney implemented a formalized collaborative model for Frozen II (2019). They formed the verddet, an advisory group comprising Sámi artists, elders, and cultural experts. This case reveals the limits of collaboration when creative authority remains non-negotiable.

Verddet: The Advisory Model

For Frozen II, Disney partnered with verddet, which included Sámi artists, reindeer herders, historians, and cultural practitioners (Arctic Council, 2020). This collaboration successfully improved visual accuracy from the generic "frozen north" aesthetic: Sámi clothing designs (gákti), reindeer practices, and other cultural details were respectfully rendered (Mihailova, 2025).

However, the musical soundscape remained firmly under Western authority. While visual consultants had meaningful input, sonic decisions were subject to negotiation, modification, and ultimately, erasure.

"Vuelie": From Exotic Atmosphere to Decontextualized Ritual

"Vuelie," the franchise's iconic opening chant, was composed by Sámi musician Frode Fjellheim in collaboration with film composer Christophe Beck. The piece mixes joik, the traditional Sámi vocal form, with classical vocal style. Originally, Frozen (2013) featured "Vuelie" for its "memorably melodic opening tune," not its cultural significance as a Sámi chant (Mihailova, 2025).

Frozen II (2019) attempted to rectify this by featuring the song in a ritual welcoming Elsa and Anna into the Northuldra community, mimicking the Sámi tradition of joiking for acceptance into society. Yet the performance still relied on the non-Sámi Cantus choir rather than Sámi voices, perpetuating what scholars term "slippage between 'Sámi' and 'Scandinavian'" and recontextualizing the ritual into background music rather than explaining its cultural significance (Mihailova, 2025).

Lead composer Beck also mischaracterizes "Vuelie" as "Hey Na Na music" and refers to joiking as a "Northern European yodel" and "Scandinavian element," obscuring its Indigenous Sámi roots in favor of vague geographic markers that erase cultural specificity (Mihailova, 2025). This failure by a key creative figure to correctly identify cultural tradition directly undermines Disney's public claims of respectful cultural stewardship.

Kulning vs. Joik: A Revealing Substitution

The film also substitutes kulning (non-Sámi Scandinavian cattle call) for joik in the mysterious voice calling Elsa, despite their distinct ethnic roots. Kulning aligns with Western expectations of "magical" Nordic fantasy; joik was deemed too unfamiliar.

Kulning was chosen to cater to Western audience expectations of what "Nordic magic" sounds like. Joik, meanwhile, has specific cultural and spiritual meanings that don't conform to generic fantasy aesthetics (Fuller, 2020). Thus, although Sámi traditions enrich the film's texture, they do not define its sonic identity. Disney only includes elements when they do not disrupt the established Disney aesthetic.
