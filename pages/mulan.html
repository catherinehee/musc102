Mulan

1998

Phase I: The Renaissance Era

Overview: Musical Orientalism and Ideological Binaries

The music of Mulan (1998) exemplifies musical Orientalism, where Disney abstracted Chinese cultural elements and reconfigured them to conform to Western commercial demands. Although the film's plot is based on Chinese folklore, the score prioritized Euro-American musical structures while using stereotypical "Asian-sounding" elements to create exotic atmosphere.

The Orientalist Musical Architecture

The score, composed by non-Chinese American Jerry Goldsmith, prioritized Western symphonic structures deemed necessary to remain "palatable" to Western audiences. This decision reveals a crucial assumption: Chinese musical traditions are insufficient for conveying universal emotion to global (read: Western) audiences.

"Honor to Us All": Pentatonic Shorthand

In the song "Honor to Us All," its composers relied on broad musical stereotypes, specifically pentatonic scales and generalized melodic phrases, which American viewers had long been conditioned to interpret as "Asian-sounding" (Amer, 2022).

Musical Binaries: East vs. West as Repression vs. Liberation

Musical Binaries

This musical architecture subtly reinforces a binary dichotomy between Eastern and Western ideologies. When Mulan pursued individualism, aligning her with progressive American values, the corresponding music, such as her solo "Reflection," was exclusively Western in style (Amer, 2022).
