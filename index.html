About This Project

This interactive timeline explores how Disney's musical representation of non-Western cultures has evolved over three decades. Through analysis of musical soundtracks, scholarly critiques, and interviews with involved musicians, we examine whether Disney's shift from exclusion to collaboration represents genuine progress or merely sophisticated adaptation of cultural hegemony.

Theoretical Framework

Orientalism

Edward Said's concept describes the systematic Western construction of non-Western peoples as exotic, inferior "Others" to assert Western superiority.

Cultural Hegemony

Antonio Gramsci's framework explains how dominant groups maintain power through ideology rather than force, normalizing beliefs that reinforce ruling systems.

Commodity Racism

bell hooks' concept where ethnicity functions as "spice, seasoning that can liven up the dull dish that is mainstream white culture" without genuine authority transfer.
